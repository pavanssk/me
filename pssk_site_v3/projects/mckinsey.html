McKinsey DCEC

McKinsey hired me to fix McKinsey.

The DCEC election process governed the promotion of Partners to Director level — one of the most consequential and politically sensitive processes inside McKinsey globally. It ran on email, spreadsheets, and institutional memory. Nine distinct stakeholder types. Six fragmented legacy systems with no integration. A four-month cycle with no programmatic version control.

The brief: map, analyse, and redesign this process. Not just the interface — the entire operational logic. Who does what, when, with what information, with what authority, with what visibility to whom.

Approach

A Master User Journey across 9 stakeholder types.

I built a comprehensive Master User Journey mapping every interaction across all nine stakeholder types — from nominating Partners through peer review, committee evaluation, final voting, and outcome communication. Each node was analysed for information requirements, decision authority, system dependencies, and failure modes.

The output identified codifiable process steps ready for automation, API integration points where legacy systems could be connected rather than replaced, and access control requirements that email fundamentally could not support.

This was pre-engineering systems analysis that would inform the architecture of whatever was built next. Consulting was the product strategy phase — I ship what I would have recommended.

Nine stakeholder types. Six fragmented systems. One process that promotes the people who run the firm. Getting this wrong has consequences.

Novartis · Good Pharma

Chief Cardiologists offered to pay for the validated prototype.

Separately, I was brought in by the Head of Digital and Head of Innovation at Novartis — both reporting to the CTO of one of the world’s top three pharmaceutical companies — to prototype digital solutions to a politically charged regulatory problem.

I led InVision prototypes, remote lab tests with clinical practitioners, and a qualitative-to-quantitative insight pipeline. The prototype was tested directly with Chief Cardiologists, Oncologists, and Dermatologists. Their response was to ask how they could pay for it to be built.
