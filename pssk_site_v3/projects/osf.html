2023 · Asset Management · London

HARGREAVES
LANSDOWN

Replacing fragmented, ad hoc operational systems with reliable Salesforce integrations at the UK’s largest retail investment platform — £100bn+ AUM, 12 weeks.

Role
UX Lead
Client
Hargreaves Lansdown via OSF Digital
Scale
£100bn+ AUM · UK’s largest retail investment platform
Context

The UK’s largest retail investment platform — running on workarounds.

Hargreaves Lansdown manages over £100 billion in assets under management for more than 1.8 million clients. Behind the consumer-facing product, cross-departmental teams were operating on a patchwork of ad hoc systems — manual processes, disconnected tools, and workflows that had grown organically rather than been designed.

OSF Digital brought me in as UX Lead to drive the design layer of a Salesforce modernisation programme — replacing the fragmented operational layer with integrated, reliable systems that could support the business at scale.

The Problem

When the back office runs on workarounds, the front office eventually feels it.

The existing workflow relied heavily on manual handoffs between departments — data entered in one system, re-entered in another, with no reliable single source of truth for client or transaction data. Staff had developed personal workarounds that worked individually but created systemic fragility.

The design challenge was twofold: first, understand the true operational workflows as they actually ran (not as they were documented), and second, design Salesforce implementations that people would actually adopt rather than work around.

In financial services at this scale, operational inefficiency isn’t just a productivity problem — it creates compliance risk, data integrity issues, and ultimately the potential for client-facing errors on accounts holding significant assets.

Understanding a workflow as it’s documented and as it actually runs are two completely different things. The gap between them is where the real design work lives.

The Approach

12 weeks. Real workflows. No assumptions.

The programme ran over 12 weeks — a tight timeline for the scope of change involved. My first priority was research: sitting with the people who actually did the work, mapping what they really did rather than what the process documents said they should do.

This produced a set of true current-state workflows — including the informal workarounds — that became the foundation for the Salesforce design. Every integration point was designed around the actual data flows and decision points that staff needed to navigate, not an idealised version of the process.

I worked closely with the Salesforce technical team to ensure the design decisions were implementable within the platform’s constraints, and with stakeholders across departments to manage the change management dimension — a new system that staff don’t trust or understand will immediately generate new workarounds.

Outcome

Reliable integrations. Streamlined workflows. A decision to go back to building.

The programme delivered reliable Salesforce integrations that replaced the most fragile manual processes and created a coherent operational layer across departments. Staff workflows were streamlined and the data handoff points that had been creating risk were consolidated.

This engagement also crystallised something personal: working this close to large-scale operational UX confirmed that I wanted to be building product, not consulting on it. I left to go back to founding and building software full-time.
