The Problem

When the AI gets the interaction wrong, someone gets hurt.

Most product design problems are forgiving. A confusing onboarding flow costs you a conversion. SOSLabs.ai operates in a different register — a user in distress, potentially in danger, relying on an AI agent to understand their situation and respond correctly. The margin for error is zero.

The core challenge: how do you design an AI that a frightened person trusts immediately? How does it signal it’s listening without requiring input? How does it communicate uncertainty without causing panic or delay? How does it hand off to emergency services at precisely the right moment?

Every interaction decision carries real-world consequence. There is no ‘iterate later’ when the product is someone’s safety.

The Approach

Designing for a user who cannot focus on their phone.

The primary constraint: the user may be unable to look at, speak to, or interact with the device. The AI must infer context from ambient audio, motion, and signal patterns. The interface exists not as a primary surface but as a reassurance layer and override mechanism.

I designed a severity classification system that surfaces progressively — from passive monitoring through gentle confirmation prompts to full emergency escalation — with each transition point requiring explicit design decisions about tone, timing, and modality.

The voice detection system required designing for distress vocalisations specifically — the acoustic signatures of panic, pain, and fear. How the system responds to a detected distress signal, and how it communicates that response, required dozens of iteration cycles against real-world edge cases.

The Build

Founding AI Software Engineer across every surface.

The platform runs on LiveKit WebRTC for zero-latency multi-device streaming, GCP and Vertex AI for cloud processing, and a neuromorphic SNN on Intel Loihi 2 for always-on edge inference at minimal power draw.

Full-stack architecture directed personally — backend (Python, FastAPI), frontend (React, TypeScript), edge (neuromorphic SNN) — with Claude, Cursor and AntiGravity executing across the stack at 10x delivery velocity.
