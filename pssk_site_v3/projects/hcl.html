The Role

Player-manager across global regulated industries.

At HCL I operated as a player-manager — leading cross-functional teams and contributing directly to product and design decisions. Teams ranged from 5 to 50 people, 80% engineers and 20% design and business analysis, working across banking, telecommunications, healthcare, and enterprise software.

Reporting to the SVP for EMEA, my remit spanned strategy, delivery, and quality — bridging senior client stakeholders and distributed engineering teams across multiple time zones. The job was to make complex things ship reliably.

Banking & Financial Services

Where failed deployments aren’t just inconvenient — they’re regulated events.

Multiple banking clients required reliable Salesforce and eCommerce deployments against stringent regulatory requirements. In financial services, a broken deployment means compliance breaches, audit trails, and in some cases regulatory reporting obligations.

I led the product and design layer across these programmes, ensuring what engineering built matched what compliance required and what end users — both internal staff and customers — could navigate without training. The design challenge: making mandatory compliance workflows feel human rather than bureaucratic.

An operations centre managing subsea cable faults cannot tolerate UI inconsistency or workflow ambiguity. Every design decision was evaluated against: does this work clearly when something is going wrong at 3am?

Liquid Telco · Subsea Cable Infrastructure

Designing for an operator laying cable across Africa and under the ocean.

Liquid Telco is one of Africa’s largest telecommunications operators, actively expanding its subsea and terrestrial fibre network across the continent — including cables running beneath the Indian and Atlantic Oceans. Infrastructure spanning 50+ countries, crews working across three continents, network operations centres monitoring thousands of kilometres of subsea cable simultaneously.

The programme involved deploying and customising enterprise systems to support this expansion — tools used by network operations teams to manage infrastructure at continental scale. Designing for this context meant understanding the operational reality of engineers in Lagos, Nairobi, Johannesburg, and London, working across vastly different connectivity environments and operational procedures.

Reliability was non-negotiable. Every design decision was evaluated against the question: does this work clearly when something is going wrong at 3am?

AI & Emerging Tech

Shipping AI-driven Metahuman chatbot systems in enterprise.

Alongside the infrastructure programmes, I led delivery of AI-driven Metahuman chatbot systems — Unreal Engine-powered photorealistic AI avatars used for customer interaction and internal knowledge management. Early enterprise AI deployment before the current wave of LLM tooling made it accessible.

Getting a photorealistic AI avatar to feel trustworthy in an enterprise context required careful design of the conversation model, visual fidelity trade-offs, and handoff protocols to human agents. The uncanny valley is a real UX problem when the stakes are customer service in a regulated industry.
