2014 · Mobile UX · London

RENTR

UX research, consultancy and UI design for a disruptive iPhone and Android rental marketplace app — from user research through to validated interface design.

Role
UX Research, Consultancy & UI Design
Platform
iOS · Android
Type
Consumer Marketplace App
The Brief

A rental marketplace with genuine disruption potential — needing design rigour.

Rentr was building a peer-to-peer rental marketplace — allowing people to rent items they owned to others nearby, and rent items they needed without buying. The concept was sound and the timing was right in the sharing economy wave. What it needed was a UX and UI layer that made the trust and transaction mechanics feel effortless on mobile.

I was brought in to conduct a full UX audit, identify the friction points in the existing flows, and redesign the interface across iOS and Android.

Research & Audit

Trust is the real UX problem in peer-to-peer marketplaces.

Peer-to-peer rental has a fundamental UX challenge that purchase marketplaces don’t: both sides of the transaction carry risk. The owner is lending something valuable. The renter is committing to return it in good condition. Neither party knows the other.

The research revealed that the existing flows weren’t adequately surfacing the trust signals that users needed to feel comfortable transacting — profile completeness, item condition documentation, review systems, and damage protection mechanisms were present but buried or inconsistently presented.

The audit also identified several friction points in the core transaction flow: item listing was too complex, the search and discovery experience didn’t adequately surface nearby items, and the booking confirmation flow lacked the reassurance signals that would reduce abandonment.

In a peer-to-peer marketplace, the interface doesn’t just need to be usable — it needs to make strangers feel safe enough to do business with each other.

Design

Reducing friction. Building trust. Shipping on both platforms.

The redesign addressed three core areas: trust signal architecture — ensuring the right confidence markers were visible at every decision point; listing simplification — reducing the cognitive load of getting an item onto the platform; and transaction flow clarity — making the booking, confirmation, and handoff process feel secure and unambiguous.

UI was designed for both iOS and Android, respecting each platform’s native patterns while maintaining a consistent Rentr brand identity across both. The visual language was clean, warm, and approachable — appropriate for a consumer product asking people to share their belongings with strangers.
