2014 — 2015 · Enterprise UX · London

GOOGLE
gTECH

Codename: Strong Coffee. Redesigning internal platforms through which over a third of Google’s global ad revenue flowed — without breaking anything.

Role
Interaction Design Lead
Team
gTech Engineering
Revenue at stake
£100bn+ annually
Context

The infrastructure through which a third of Google’s revenue flowed.

gTech Engineering owned all mission-critical internal tooling at Google — the operational backbone used by sales teams, finance, and engineering to monitor and manage the ad revenue systems that fund everything Google does. If this breaks, Google’s revenue visibility breaks.

The brief: merge two platforms — gStatus (real-time revenue alerting) and Pelican (ad revenue analytics) — into a single coherent experience, during a live infrastructure overhaul, without disrupting the teams who depended on these tools daily.

The constraint: make mission-critical data legible to a non-technical sales director who should never need to write a Dremel query.

The Design Problem

Complex SQL workflows — made invisible.

Pelican’s existing workflow required users to write Dremel queries (Google’s internal equivalent of SQL at scale) to get meaningful revenue data. This created a significant expertise barrier — the people who most needed the data were least equipped to retrieve it.

The solution was a layered drill-down interface: high-level revenue signals visible immediately, with progressive disclosure into granularity. No query. No export. No waiting for an analyst.

gStatus required a different challenge — designing for anomaly detection and alert triage at scale. When a revenue signal goes red at 11pm, the on-call engineer needs to understand scope, probable cause, and affected systems at a glance.

Process

Engineering around constraints and bypass mechanisms.

The infrastructure overhaul created a significant constraint: we were designing across conflicting frontend frameworks being migrated simultaneously. To maintain design fidelity, I personally engineered custom CSS and TypeScript components to bypass restrictive internal systems, delivering a clean, cohesive Material Design experience for £100bn+ ad-revenue dashboards. I also ran human-centred sprint prioritisation sessions with Senior Management and Engineering — keeping design grounded in what users actually needed rather than what was technically convenient to build.
