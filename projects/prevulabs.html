The Problem

Cryptographic media identity — made human.

Content creators face an increasingly hostile environment: AI-generated deepfakes, stolen footage, disputed ownership, identity fraud. Pre.VU was built to solve this at the infrastructure level — a proprietary cryptographic hashing and temporal security protocol that creates unforgeable cryptographic proof of media origin.

The design challenge: this technology is genuinely complex. The interaction design job was to make it completely invisible — creators should feel protected, not educated. Trust signals without technical jargon. Security without friction.

The Approach

Multi-device, multi-surface, simultaneous — and still simple.

Pre.VU operates across devices simultaneously — shooting on iPhone, monitoring on iPad, reviewing on desktop, all in one session. The interaction design had to solve for state clarity across heterogeneous hardware in real time: which device is active, what’s being protected, what’s the sync status.

I designed a custom AI Director dashboard — a real-time contextual analysis layer giving creators intelligence about their content as it’s being made: composition, identity verification status, sync health, and quality audit signals.

Onboarding was a particular focus: a creator who doesn’t understand cryptography needs to feel safe within 60 seconds of first opening the app.

The interaction design challenge: make complex AI behaviour feel simple and legible to someone who should never need to think about it.

The Rebuild

Flutter to native — a complete rebuild at 10x velocity.

The original app was built in Flutter. Performance demands and the granular control required for real-time media processing required a full rebuild in Native iOS Swift and Native Android Kotlin. I executed this rebuild alone using Cursor as an accelerant — maintaining design fidelity across platforms while improving the underlying architecture.

The backend — a Python/FastAPI system handling the hashing protocol, stream management, and AI analysis pipeline — was also redesigned during this period on GCP.
