The Engagement

Full design ownership — from identity to conversion.

Gumsaver was a consumer product requiring a complete design engagement: not just UI, but the full stack from brand identity through to growth mechanics. This meant owning the visual identity, product UX, interface design, SEO architecture, and conversion rate optimisation as a unified brief.

This kind of engagement — where design decisions at the brand level directly affect the conversion mechanics at the bottom of the funnel — requires holding all the layers simultaneously. A brand choice affects SEO. A UX decision affects CRO. Getting this wrong at any layer creates drag across all the others.

Brand & Identity

Building a brand that earns trust at first glance.

The brand work established the visual language, tone of voice, and identity system that would carry through every touchpoint — digital and physical. For a consumer product, the brand is often the primary trust signal before a user has any experience of the product itself.

The identity needed to be distinctive enough to be memorable, approachable enough to convert first-time visitors, and robust enough to work across product UI, marketing materials, and print.

UX, SEO & CRO

Designing for discovery as well as use.

The UX work encompassed the core product flows and the marketing site — with SEO and CRO considerations baked in from the start rather than retrofitted. Information architecture decisions directly affect organic search performance. Page structure decisions directly affect conversion rates. Treating these as separate workstreams produces worse outcomes across all of them.

The CRO work identified the specific moments in the user journey where intent was highest and friction was causing drop-off — and redesigned those moments to remove the obstacles between a user’s motivation and completing the action.

Brand, UX, SEO, and CRO are not separate disciplines. They’re the same decision viewed from different angles.
