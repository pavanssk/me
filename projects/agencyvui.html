The Origin

Building before the tools existed.

I founded Agency VUi in 2008 — the year the App Store launched — which meant building mobile-first digital products before the ecosystem to support them properly existed. No Flutter, no React Native, no Expo. Java for Android. PHP and MySQL on the backend. Everything built from first principles.

The agency grew to serve clients across Education, Health, and Environment — sectors where the design challenge was as much about convincing people that digital products could help them as it was about building the products.

NFC Innovation

Proximity interaction — five years before Apple let anyone do it.

Between 2009 and 2012, I built NFC-powered (Near Field Communication) experiences for a student collaboration platform developed with Nottingham University. What made this notable: Apple didn’t enable NFC access for third-party developers until 2017. We were building on Android, in 2009, doing things that wouldn’t be possible on iPhone for another eight years.

The application was an EdTech take on university lectures — NFC check-ins for lecture attendance, proximity-triggered content sharing between students, and location-aware collaboration built around the physical space of a campus. The concept attracted investment interest from the University of Nottingham.

We were building proximity interaction experiences in 2009 that Apple’s users couldn’t replicate until 2017. Sometimes being early is the whole point.

Growth & Validation

£10k grant. £40k investment offer. A lesson in what matters.

Agency VUi secured a £10,000 grant for innovation in digital product development and received a £40,000 investment offer from North West tech board investors.

I brought all development in-house after outsourced code failed to scale to enterprise clients. If you can’t read it, you can’t own it. If you don’t own it, you can’t control what ships. That lesson has informed every technical decision I’ve made since.
